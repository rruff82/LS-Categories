Item 5.

\(\mathbb{R}_{\geq 0} \xrightarrow{l} \mathbb{R}_{\geq 0}\) with \(l(x)=\frac{1}{x+1}\) feels like a trick question. For most intents and purposes, we should have \(l^{-1}(x)=\frac{1}{x}-1\) but the domain and codomain don't look right here. Specifically, we have a problem where \(l^{-1}(0)\) is undefined and another problem where \(l^{-1}(x) < 0\) for \(x > 1\text{.}\) Based on these observations, I'd have to conclude that \(l\) is not invertable.
