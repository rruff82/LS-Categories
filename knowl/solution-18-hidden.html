Part (a) is given in the text, so lets focus on (b). If \(h \circ f = k \circ f\text{,}\) it follows that \((h \circ f) \circ f^{-1} = (k \circ f) \circ f^{-1}\text{.}\)
By the associative property, \((h \circ f) \circ f^{-1} = h \circ (f \circ f^{-1})\) and \(f \circ f^{-1} = 1_B\text{.}\) Thus, \((h \circ f) \circ f^{-1} = h \circ 1_B = h\text{.}\) By similar reasoning, \((k \circ f) \circ f^{-1} = k \circ (f \circ f^{-1}) = k \circ 1_B = k\text{.}\)
It follows that \((h \circ f) \circ f^{-1} = (k \circ f) \circ f^{-1}\) if and only if \(h = k\) which completes our proof.
