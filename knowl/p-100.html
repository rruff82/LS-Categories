Notice how the graph of \(\frac{1}{x}-1\) seemingly has an asymptote at \(x=0\) and gets "cut-off" when it hits the x-axis. For us to consider this as an inverse map, the domain of the inverse should match the codomain of the map and vice versa. Even though these maps meet my reflection criteria, the fact that \(\mathbb{R}_{\geq 0} \xrightarrow{l^{-1}} \mathbb{R}_{\geq 0}\) is undefined for \(x = 0\) and \(x > 1\) when domain and codomain are supposed to both be \(\mathbb{R}_{\geq 0}\) disqualifies it from being considered an inverse to the map \(\mathbb{R}_{\geq 0} \xrightarrow{l} \mathbb{R}_{\geq 0}\text{.}\)
